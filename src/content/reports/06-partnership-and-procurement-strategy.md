## Brand and entity structure

The customer-facing entity should be Canadian-incorporated, with a Canadian operating leadership team and Canadian board representation. Ownership and origin should be transparent — opacity creates more risk than it removes.

## Partner archetypes

1. **Provincial utilities (ON, QC, BC)** — primary partners for managed charging and depot programs. Master cooperation agreements should be signed early.
2. **Site hosts** — retail, fuel, hospitality, fleet operators, multi-unit residential developers.
3. **Electrical contractors and EPCs** — regional framework agreements for installation and commissioning.
4. **System integrators** — for BESS deployment, especially when local content matters.
5. **Capital partners** — pension capital, infrastructure funds, and the Canada Infrastructure Bank for project finance.

## Procurement and security review

For any public-sector or critical-infrastructure work, expect:

- National-security and supply-chain review of hardware and software provenance.
- Data residency and cybersecurity attestations.
- Local-content and labour expectations.

This is not a barrier — it is a checklist. Plan for it from day one and lead with it in conversations.
