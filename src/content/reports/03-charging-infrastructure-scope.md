## Home and multi-unit L2

Home charging covers the majority of EV energy delivered. The opportunity is a hardware + install + smart panel bundle, sold through dealer, retail, and utility channels. Margins are modest but the channel is sticky and lead-rich.

Multi-unit residential is structurally underserved — most Canadian condos and apartments have neither metering infrastructure nor wiring. A turnkey package (panel upgrade, sub-meter, charger, billing software) is a defensible niche.

## Public DC fast charging

Highway corridors are the most visible segment but also the most competitive. The right play is curated urban + corridor coverage paired with retail anchors (food, retail, fuel), not a race to add ports on top of existing operators.

Site economics depend on:

- Average daily energy delivered per stall.
- Demand charges and time-of-use exposure.
- Whether BESS is co-located.
- Whether the operator owns the software and billing layer.

## Fleet depot

Highest near-term ROI segment for an infrastructure-first entrant. Fleets offer:

- Predictable, contracted utilization.
- Single-counterparty commercial relationships.
- Natural pull-through for BESS, telematics, and managed charging software.

Targets include municipal transit, last-mile logistics, regional utilities, and mid-size logistics operators.

## Software and O&M

The defensible long-term layer. A modern CPMS (charge point management system) plus telemetry, billing, and field service stack — hosted in a Canadian region with OCPP 2.0.1 and ISO 15118 — can be operated for owned sites in year one and offered to third-party site hosts from year two onward.

This is where ecosystem economics live: hardware-agnostic software with high gross margin and recurring revenue.

## Battery storage (BESS)

BESS pairs naturally with DCFC and depot charging, defers grid upgrades, and unlocks ancillary market participation in select provinces. It is also the most natural place to deploy Chinese cell technology through a local integrator structure, since the customer-facing value is the integrated service, not the cell origin.
